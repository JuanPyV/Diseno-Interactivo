What is alcohol use disorder?

Alcohol use disorder is when you have problems controlling your drinking, you feel worry when you don't drink it, continue drinking it even if it causes you problems or having withdrawal symptoms when you rapidly decrease or stop drinking. This disorder includes any type of alcohol that puts your health and safety in risk, or when you drink a lot of alcohol where a male consumes five or more drinks within two hours or a female at least four drinks within two hours. Alcohol dependence (alcoholism) is a disorder of compulsive drinking associated with impaired control over intake, such as repeatedly exceeding self-defined limits, a persistent desire to quit or cut down and difficulty doing so, and continued use despite adverse consequences. (Upsatine, 2013)

Why you become alcoholic?

There are several reasons why the alcohol use disorder appear: one of them is because they want to run away from their reality, no matter what the situation, if it is a conflict, it also helps them to resist fears, authority(especially from the father) and people who don't love them , because they are afraid to reveal themselves. Drinking alcohol can give a feeling of power and strength.

When do you know you are alcoholic?

When you begin enable to limit how much alcohol you drink, spend a lot time drinking, feeling a strong urge to drink, don't going to work or school due to repeated alcohol use, continue drinking even if you know it is causing physical, social or interpersonal problems, reducing social and work activities and hobbies. All this are some symptoms that you are getting into an alcohol use disorder.

Who can be alcoholic?

Alcohol use disorder is a heritable disease: Approximately 50% of the risk is genetic, while environmental factors account for the remainder, the most clearly established factor being early childhood abuse or neglect. Multigenerational alcohol dependence often appears in the early to mid-teens, but functional alcohol dependence can begin at any age.

How do you become alcoholic?

There are a lot of factor that are involved in the possibility of being an alcoholic, such as genetic, psychological, social and environmental, because they affect your behavior. Some doctors suggest that some alcoholic drinks have a different and stronger impact, that obviously lead to a alcoholic use disorder.
