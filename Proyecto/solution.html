Benchmark Solutions

“Salvia divinorum”
One of the important and interesting solutions I found, as a way of helping with alcoholism, was the use of a species of plant called “Salvia divinorum”, used in a ritual that should help with cases of abuse and addiction of pretty much everything, so using it to cure this problem could work. They believe that the feminine entity that inhabits the plant heals the body, teaches the patient what causes and sustains the addiction, and guides the patient on the road to recovery and a balanced life.

"Enforce a minimum price for alcohol"
One of the most known solutions that have been tried, was raising the price of alcohol so people would think twice before buying an expensive bottle of wine, tequila, or any variant of beverage that includes this substance. It has proven inefficient all the time it’s been tried because of how people that have fallen into addiction will not stop even for a higher price to buy what they think they need.

Health problems

This problem is quite evident, leading to possible injuries on one-self, to other people that have nothing to do with that problem, and other kind of injuries that can happen while intoxicated. Alcohol poisoning is one of the most common ones that can happen, especially when dealing with people that don’t drink too often and because of that they exceed on what they drink, leading to the possibility of them dying because of the lack of information on this theme. Physical scars may fade with time, but the psychological ones often stay with you all of your life; how would you feel knowing that you accidentally ended the life of an innocent family?

Legal consequences

Being in an intoxicated state can end up causing you all sorts of legal problems, as being in this kind of state, makes you more prone to being aggressive, or making rash decisions in the moment, without thinking of all the possible consequences of your acts. Alcohol-related offences include assault, sexual assault, assault of police, property damage, disorderly or offensive behaviour, hindering police, resisting arrest and drinkdriving.
